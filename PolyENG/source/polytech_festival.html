Polytech Festival 2015

May 23−24, 2015, VDNKh Industry Square, Moscow

Once again, just like last year, the main Moscow spring event in popular science will be the Polytech Festival, gathering scientists and artists from Russia, the US, the Netherlands, Sweden, France and Poland. An open-air festival will take place on Industry Square and in the nearby VDNKh pavilions. Polytech Festival aims to make science more popular and attractive for youngsters and to involve them in it. Discussions, interactive installations, exhibitions, street performances, invention presentations, lectures and even a dynamic attraction — all these and many other activities are scheduled for the Polytech Festival 2015.

Pep Bou

Show, Spain, 2014

Pep Bou is a prominent Catalan illusionist and juggler, famous for his soap bubble shows. In Moscow, he is going to present his program Bereshit, which tells the story of the origins of the Universe. Jorge Wagensberg, a distinguished expert in museography and the founder of the museum of science CosmoCaixa Barcelona, takes to the stage as the narrator of the story.

Car Organ

Sound installation, US, 2010

Car Organ is a collaborative project between the Polytechnic Museum and SYYNLabs (US). Twenty cars are attached to a keyboard with their horns tempered equally. Every Festival visitor has a chance to play the car organ, which represents the synthesis of science, art and technology. The Festival will feature an electronic music performance as well.

Gap Filling

Street performance, Russia–Poland, 2010

The performance by the engineering theater AKHE stages stories of unrelated characters, taking place simultaneously in six room boxes.

Rings

Interactive architectural installation, the Netherlands, 2014

This cardboard installation was designed by Noa Haim. Large in scale — between 3.5 and 5.5 meters tall — it will be displayed in the hall of VDNKh pavilion 26, where every festivalgoer will have a chance to join the construction process. Noa Haim has developed the form of the sculpture and the ornament design based on the Polytechnic Museum’s logo especially for the Polytech Festival.

TOUCHY (A Human Camera)

Invention, Hong Kong, 2014

A student from Hong Kong invented this camera helmet to make picking up girls easier. The camera’s pupil-like lenses are open only during tactile contact with another person. If the contact lasts more than 10 seconds, the camera takes a picture.

TOUCHY was one of the highlights of the Austrian festival Ars Electronica 2014. At the Polytech Festival, the inventor Eric Siu is going to establish tactile connection with visitors to VDNKh.

PONG

Exhibition, Museum of Videogames

The Museum of Videogames, a project by Museum of Soviet Arcade Machines, will bring its stand to the festival. The stand highlights the Pong video game — a table tennis simulator created in 1968.

Here you can face the Kuka industrial robot and try to defeat it — last year, it challenged former world champion Timo Boll to a battle! A part of the museum’s collection of retro game consoles and arcade machines will also be displayed at the stand — come and see!

Sport Machines

Exhibition, Museum of Soviet Arcade Machines

Prepare for a journey — you are going to travel 40 years back to find yourself in an ancient amusement park: table football, hockey and basketball on arcade machines from the museum’s collection.

Soviet vending machines will tirelessly pour out sparkling water and kvass, change money, sell newspapers and prepare oxygen cocktails.

Water Light Graffiti

Interactive installation, France, 2013

The water light graffiti by French artist Antonin Fourneau — an enormous LED wall, where everyone can draw with water — will be brought to Moscow for the first time for the festival. Using a wet brush, sponge or just pouring water on the wall (and thereby closing tiny electrical circuits), you can draw a portrait or a landscape — or just write your name!

Beat Blox

Interactive installation, Sweden, 2014

Beat Blox is a musical device by Per Holmkvist, made up of three turntables, simulating the sound of a bongo, bass guitar and drum respectively. To play the Beat Blox, you should move, replace or add small wooden blocks to the surface of the turntable. Sensors detect the distance between the blocks, making the Arduino processors, which are embedded in the turntables, play various sound loops. At the Festival, Beat Bloxs will be displayed as an interactive object, so every visitor will have a chance to play it.

60 Flavors

Interactive Installation, Austria, 2014

Ever thought of literally tasting the scale of corruption around the world? Artist Ulrich Lantzberg has created a mechanism that transforms the information about corruption growth in different countries into various flavors. A visitor picks up a candy that is then filled up with a certain flavor corresponding to the corruption level in some particular region. One thousand candies will be prepared for the Polytech Festival.

Concerto No. 2 in C minor, Op. 18 for pianola and orchestra by Sergei Rachmaninoff

Concert lecture, Russia, 2015

Pianola is a mechanism that turns a regular piano into an automatic one. Powered by a bellows system, pneumatics and a punch tape, these instruments were extremely popular through the late 19th and early 20th centuries, and Sergei Rachmaninoff himself was a brand ambassador for Ampico, a company which specialized in producing player pianos. The composer wrote over thirty music pieces for punch tapes, including his famous Piano Concerto No. 2. In the concert lecture, the audience will learn a lot about the history and mechanism of pianolas, and will hear a concert piece by Rachmaninoff performed on the instrument.

Periscope

Interactive installation, Russia, 2015

A periscope is not only an instrument for observation under cover, but also something that changes an observer’s point of view. Portable cardboard periscopes will be specially created for the festival. Participants will put on the periscopes and try to accomplish simple tasks like scoring a basket, playing frisbee, or touching another person. See how they will manage these challenges!

Metamorphoses

Exhibition, Finland–Russia, 2013

Twenty figures of insects and mythical creatures play with the notion of growing up. Changes that children go through while growing up correspond to the insect transformation. The figures in the installation are somewhat between children and insects at different stages of development. Artist: Alexander Reichstein, audio effects: Petri Laakso, sound artist from Finland.

SСI-KU

Performance, Russia, 2015

Especially for the Festival, SKVT project teenage participants directed by philologist Alexey Stekhov and microbiologist Anton Zakharov will create three-line poetry sci-ku about the chaos theory, the origin of the Universe and sensory perception.

Sci-ku is three-line poetry about the world around us, composed by the rules of Japanese haiku. It is indeed a new trend in children’s and pop-science literature!

Waterwall

Dynamic attraction, Russia, 2014

To what extent do you trust modern technology? Waterwall is a collaborative project between the Polytechnic Museum and the MODIS Lab of MSU. Here is the challenge: Water falls down from the doorway. If you run and jump at it, the water stops, but if you are more hesitant the water pours all over you. So, do you dare to try?
